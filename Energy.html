What is Energy?

Energy is a physical quantity that represents the capacity to perform work. It exists in various forms such as kinetic energy (movement), potential energy (stored), thermal (heat), electrical, chemical, and nuclear energy. The SI unit of energy is Joule (J).

Energy FAQs

The SI unit of energy is Joule (J).

1 Calorie ≈ 4.184 Joules.

Energy refers to the total capacity to do work, while Power is the rate at which energy is used per unit time.

Electricity consumption is billed in Kilowatt-hours (kWh).

Renewable energy (such as solar, wind, and hydro) can be naturally replenished, while Non-renewable energy (coal, oil, gas) is limited and eventually depletes.
