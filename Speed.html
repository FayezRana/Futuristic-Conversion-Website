What is Speed?

Speed is the rate at which an object covers distance in a given time. It tells us how fast something is moving regardless of its direction. For example, if a car travels 60 kilometers in one hour, its speed is 60 km/h.

Speed FAQs

The SI unit of speed is meters per second (m/s).

The formula of speed is: Speed = Distance ÷ Time.

Speed is a scalar quantity (only magnitude), while Velocity is a vector quantity (magnitude + direction).

No, speed cannot be negative. It is always zero or positive because distance cannot be negative.

Common units of speed include m/s (meters per second), km/h (kilometers per hour), and mph (miles per hour).
