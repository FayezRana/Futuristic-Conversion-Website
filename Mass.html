What is Mass?

Mass is a fundamental property of matter that represents the amount of substance or matter in an object. It is different from weight, as weight depends on gravity, but mass remains constant everywhere in the universe. The SI unit of mass is kilogram (kg). Other common units include gram (g), tonne (t), and pound (lb).

Mass FAQs

The SI unit of mass is kilogram (kg).

Mass is the amount of matter in an object, while weight is the force exerted by gravity on that mass. Weight changes with gravity, but mass does not.

Instruments like a balance scale, electronic scale, and spring balance are commonly used to measure mass.

Mass is a scalar quantity because it has magnitude but no direction.

Mass is essential in physics because it determines an object’s inertia, momentum, energy, and gravitational attraction.
