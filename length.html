What is Length?

Length is a physical quantity that measures the distance between two points. It is a fundamental dimension in physics and everyday life. The SI unit of length is meter (m). Other commonly used units include centimeter (cm), kilometer (km), and inch.

Length FAQs

The SI unit of length is meter (m).

Instruments like ruler, measuring tape, vernier caliper, and meter scale are commonly used.

Common units include millimeter (mm), centimeter (cm), meter (m), kilometer (km), inch, and foot.

Length is a scalar quantity because it has only magnitude and no direction.

Measuring length is essential in construction, science experiments, engineering, navigation, and daily life tasks.
