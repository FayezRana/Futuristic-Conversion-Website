Temperature Definition

Temperature is a measure of the hotness or coldness of a substance. It represents the average kinetic energy of the particles in that substance. Higher kinetic energy means a higher temperature, while lower kinetic energy means a lower temperature.

Commonly used temperature scales are:

  • Celsius (°C): Water freezing = 0°C, boiling = 100°C
  • Fahrenheit (°F): Water freezing = 32°F, boiling = 212°F
  • Kelvin (K): Scientific scale, 0 K = Absolute Zero
